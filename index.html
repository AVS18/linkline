The Future of 4 Wheels


   The past decade has been a wake-up call for almost every country on earth, natural disasters and calamities have increased at an unprecedented rate in every country in the world. Experts in the field attribute this change in the climate and the ecological balance to the rapidly developing industries and pollution from the same. One of the main contributors to this pollution is the automotive industry that burns gasoline and diesel to power vehicles used by almost everybody on earth. Realising that burning these fuels and releasing carbon and other polluting substances into the atmosphere will lead to our inevitable doom, the automotive industry started looking into alternative sources of energy to power vehicles for transportation.

   The leading contender in the race is electric energy. Over the past decade, we have seen great advancements in the field of battery technology and electric motors that have brought Electric Vehicles (EV) closer to feasibility than ever before. One of the main issues that plagued the EV industry is the widespread existence of range anxiety. People are concerned that an EV might leave them stranded in the middle of nowhere if they take it on a long drive. In the case of gasoline or diesel-powered car, if you run out of fuel, you could walk to a gas station and purchase a bottle of fuel to pour into the fuel tank and have the car drive to the fuel station. While in an EV you would have to call a tow truck to come and tow the vehicle to the charging station where it would have to spend a few hours to regain the charge while the fuel-powered cars can have their complete range restored in a matter of minutes. Another issue that consumers fear is the extended charging times needed in order to completely replenish the battery. While petrol or diesel-powered car can go from 0 to a few hundred kilometres of range in a matter of minutes, an EV would take at least an hour even with the most advanced charging systems out there.


   Tesla, one of the most leading companies in battery technology and the automotive industry has pioneered a wide variety of solutions to the issues that the customers fear and which in turn lead to hesitation in purchasing an electric vehicle. Unlike the initial years when an EV battery was inherently a very large lead-acid battery where the range and charging were both poor, the modern cars come with a lithium-ion battery that is capable of long-range travel and faster-charging speeds. Tesla uses individual Lithium-ion cells to power their vehicles, it’s basically hundreds of normal batteries that are found in daily use items like TV remote stacked together to form a large battery. This helps in repair and increases the durability of the entire battery.

   There have been many experiments by different manufacturers to figure out how to make mass consumer EV which can be daily driven and taken on long trips without the fear being stranded. Some of the concepts being investigated or hybrid engines where a small displacement gas powered engine is aided by an electric motor to increase performance and efficiency, another concept being investigated is the idea of swapping batteries at stations to instantly regain drivable range. This concept quickly met a roadblock when they realised it would require standardising the shape size and specification of all batteries in all electric vehicles, deeming this concept not feasible. One of the other solutions adopted on a large scale is the use of high power DC charging. Conventional chargers use an AC to DC converter onboard the vehicle to charge the car, while DC chargers do the conversion outside allowing for larger converters which can produce more power. This technology is still in its infancy and isn’t widespread enough to assure the consumers of worry free travel.

   A major change brought on by the adoption of Electric vehicles that most people overlook is the change in the structure of the car. The heavy battery that is located so close to the ground leads to a low centre of gravity that leads to improved stability around a corner. The increased weight will also require the chassis to be stronger leading to a more stronger body that can will lead to more safety. Currently with the advancements of battery technology and DC charging, widespread use of electric vehicles is definitely the future of personal transportation and is expected to be adopted completely within the next few decades.

- Arjun Sunil Kumar, RIET


ABOUT IEEE LINK

   From promoting the eclectic interests of students to guiding them technically, the IEEE LINK has completed yet another fruitful year amidst all the Covid hustle. The Student Activities Committee presided by the Chair, with 105 Student Branches at its toe, was able to witness a tremendous increase in the membership count as well as the participation of students in various activities.


LINK F2F

   The IEEE LINK F2F 2021 was conducted virtually on 26th January 2021 on the platform Zoom. The event saw the participation of 140 students from 68 Student Branches across Kerala. Ms. Sreelakshmi M Rajeev (Student Representative, IEEE Kerala Section) delivered the official Welcome Address, followed by the IEEE Code of Ethics delivered by Ms. Anuvinda Krishnan (WiE Coordinator, IEEE LINK).

   The event was officially inaugurated by Smt. Sarada Jayakrishnan (Chair, IEEE Kerala Section). The inaugural address was followed by a session by Prof. Muhammed Kasim S (Vice-Chair, IEEE Kerala Section) who addressed the new leaders by sharing the legacy of the IEEE Kerala Section and our SBs. This was followed by felicitation by Dr Gilesh M P (Secretary, IEEE Kerala Section) who highlighted the necessity of proper documentation of the events conducted and explained briefly about the VTools reporting and maintaining an event calendar.

   This session was followed by a talk on the SB Financial Guidelines by Dr Biju K (Treasurer, IEEE Kerala Section). Following the financial guidelines, Mr. Gopakumar S (Advisor, SAC) felicitated the event by addressing the gathering. The panel discussion was followed by a session on SB operations and Guidelines by Prof.Shankar J (Chair, SAC) who introduced the newly elected LINK and SAC teams. The session also discussed the general guidelines to be followed by the SBs and the members, the guidelines on SB operations and addressed a few queries regarding the same.

   This session was followed by appreciating those SBs who grabbed the winning titles of the various initiatives by the LINK as well as the corresponding Hub teams, namely the Cluster Wars (pre-event to the Hub Meets) and TRANSCEND, a membership development initiative. Smt. Sarada Jayakrishnan did the honors by revealing the winners.


   The forenoon session of LINK F2F 2021 was wrapped up by 1.30 PM, with a Vote of Thanks delivered by Mr. Melvin Thomas (Technical Coordinator, IEEE LINK). The afternoon session commenced with a session on ‘Guidelines on collaborating with the IEEE Young Professionals with Student Activities’ by Prof. Nandan S (Chair, IEEE YP, IEEE Kerala Section).



   This session was followed by a session on VTools reporting and OU analytics by Mr. Thomas Sabu (Membership Development Chair, IEEE Kerala Section) who explained in detail about VTools reporting and the procedures that follow. Overall, the LINK F2F 2021 proved to be a platform for the newly inducted office-bearers to get to know each other, receive a lead on planning and executing various programs in their respective SBs, the various opportunities IEEE provide for its members, and finally to aid them in bringing the best out of all our volunteers.


Virtual VOLT for LINK Team

   Volunteer Leadership Training for the Student Team was held virtually on 10th & 11th February from 7.30-9.30 PM through Google meet platform. The session was handled by Mr. Nandan S (Chair, IEEE YP, IEEE Kerala Section), he explained the responsibilities of each of the LINK Team members individually as well as overall as a team.

   General guidelines to be followed while interacting with the officials and other fellow members were also discussed. On the second day, the student team discussed various events and new initiatives they are planning to conduct this year.

Kerala Section Student Branch Chairs Meet #1

   The All Kerala SB Chairs Meet was conducted virtually on 12th February 2021 on Zoom. The event saw the participation of over 75 chairs from SBs around Kerala. The event brought together the IEEE Kerala SB Chairs and the LINK Team, and the general guidelines on SB collaboration and Science Week Celebrations 2021 were discussed.

   The event commenced with an opening remark by Ms. Sreelakshmi M Rajeev (Student Representative, IEEE Kerala Section) to welcome the gathering. The ice-breaking session was followed by a session by Prof.Shankar J (Chair, SAC) who discussed the various ways in which SBs can collaborate with each other and with the Section and Hub teams.

   It was highlighted that SBs collaborating with each other should mandatorily inform the respective HSRs and VSACs about the same. This was followed by the discussion on Science Week Celebrations 2021 by Mr. Melvin Thomas (Technical Coordinator, IEEE LINK).

   The Chairs were asked to take part in the LINK Science Week Celebrations from 21st – 28th February 2021, by organizing events and competitions based on the theme: “Science in everyday life”. The SBs were asked to submit a consolidated report of the same on or before 2nd March 2021 in accordance with the report template provided by the LINK Team.

   Next up was the discussion on procedures for sharing the posters and write-ups of SB events through the Social Media handles of LINK, initiated by Mr. Joel John Kandathil (ECC, IEEE LINK). A mail with all the discussed information about Science Week and the design guidelines was sent to all the SB Chairs soon after the event.

   All the Chairs were actively involved in the discussion and all the doubts that came up were addressed by the SAC and VSACs. After the doubt-clearing session, the SB Chairs Meet was officially wrapped up and a virtual group photograph was taken.

Execom Trainings

   The main objective behind the training program for Execom members is to get the delegates learn the essential ins and outs of managing an OU – from initial stage to a well established SB and what’s in between. Along with that, the Execom needed to understand about the awards and funds provided by IEEE. That is, giving a deep insight into the activities held at all levels of the organization.

   The delegates who participated in the program were introduced by any of the members of LINK Team . Then the speaker dives into deeper topics such as email and telephone etiquettes, beginning from defining what an OU is. Queries were taken from the volunteer leaders then, and were rectified by the speaker. For further contact details of the LINK/MD team were given for the support.

   The event stretched about 1.5-2 hours, concluded with a small interactive session at the end. Total 20 Execom Training Sessions have been conducted so far.

Membership Development Session

   IEEE LINK Membership Development team conducted Membership Develop ment sessions for COLLEGE OF ENGINEERING KALLOOPPARA, AHALIA SCHOOL OF ENGINEERING AND TECHNOLOGY, PALAKKAD, TKMIT ,CENTRAL UNIVERSITY OF KERALA and MES COLLEGE OF ENGINEERING KUTTIPPURAM.

   The main aim of the session was to introduce students to IEEE, what IEEE could provide you, the various benefits of IEEE Student Membership, steps to take student membership, volunteering opportunities offered by IEEE.

   It was a very fruitful session and was able to attract young minds to join IEEE. The main focus of the event was to give awareness aboutIEEE membership thereby promoting student membership and also in increasing the retention rate.

Science Week Celebrations

   February 28th is celebrated as National Science Day, Commemorating the discovery of the “Raman effect” by Sir C.V Raman. To mark this important event and to encourage the upcoming generations in the field of STEM, IEEE LINK decided to have a one-week celebration as Science Week from February 21st-28th, 2021.

   The Student Branches across the section organized various activities such as technical quizzes, debates, paper presentations, workshops, and online webinars and made this one-week span celebration a great success.

   The event had great support from the whole student community and had active participation in every SB. The Student Branches were encouraged to conduct events among their members to conduct events such as Tech Talks, Debates, Competitions, quizzes, technical writing, etc. Reports were submitted regarding all the events conducted as part of the science week by respective student branches. Active participation and a total of 160+ events were conducted.
